Territory view — where the white space is

The same assessed-door universe as the account map, viewed geographically. Each state card shows white-space volume and rate, the displacement-vs-greenfield split, and the leading tracked competitor — the deploy-the-reps question of rate versus volume.

▸ Rate vs volume · where to deploy

New Jersey carries the highest white-space RATE (68.9% of assessed doors) — the most efficient territory per rep. New York is the highest VOLUME (189 open doors) — the biggest absolute prize. Illinois is the most penetrated (53.4% white space, the lowest) — Curaleaf already holds the most shelf there. Rate tells you efficiency; volume tells you scale.

▸ Denominator & reconciliation

Per-state white space is measured against that state's assessed doors (dispensaries with enough priced shelf data to classify). State assessed/Curaleaf/rate are derived from the door-level records; the displacement/greenfield/competitor counts come from the payload's summary.by_state. The three states sum to the pilot total: 287 white-space doors of 450 assessed. This is the same 287 shown on the account map — one dataset, two lenses.
