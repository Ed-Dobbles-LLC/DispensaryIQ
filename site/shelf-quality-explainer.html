What are shelf-quality metrics, and how do we measure them?

Three observable signals separate brands that bought distribution from brands that earn merchandising. Each metric is derived from dispensary-menu data — no survey input, no operator self-reporting. Here's exactly what each one measures, with worked examples on stylized menus.

Metric 1 · Shelf score

Where in the menu does the brand land?

Definition. For each Curaleaf product, we record its position in section (rank 1, 2, 3 ...) and the section total (how many products in that category). Shelf score = 100 × (1 − position / section total). We average across every observed Curaleaf product.

Reading. 100 = top of every section. 50 = middle. 1 = always at the bottom. Higher is better.

Why it matters. Shoppers scroll. Above-the-fold and top-of-section position drives conversion. The brand at position 1 of 30 gets meaningfully more clicks than the brand at position 28 of 30 — even if the SKU is identical.

Caveat. Some menus alphabetize, some sort by margin, some by featured-status, some by inventory. Across thousands of stores the noise averages out and the rank signal stays clean.

Metric 2 · Promo intensity

How aggressively is the brand discounted?

Definition. Percentage of a brand's SKUs that carry an active discount on capture day. We detect a discount whenever a menu displays an explicit % off, an MSRP-vs-sale split, or strikethrough pricing.

Reading. Real numbers in the pilot range from 2% (premium brands like Off Hours) to 50%+ (NJ deep-discount operators like the_essence). Curaleaf pilot sits at 17.7% — moderate.

Why it matters. Promo intensity reads two ways. Low intensity may be premium positioning, but it may also be weak promotional activation. High intensity may signal aggressive market push, but it can also mean retailers discounting to move slow inventory. The actionable read comes from comparing your brands to the field in the same state, same category, same week.

Limitation. Discount data is populated on 14% of captured rows — some platforms surface promo info, some don't. Numbers are directional within state but not strictly comparable across all menus.

Metric 3 · Featured share

Editorial endorsement — Staff Pick, Featured, Manager's Choice

Definition. Percentage of a brand's observed SKUs that carry an editorial flag on the menu — "Staff Pick," "Featured," "Manager's Choice," "Editor's Pick," or platform equivalents. The dispensary chose to surface this SKU above the algorithmic default.

Reading. Rare signal. Pilot averages run 0.3–2% for most brands. Where it fires, it's strong evidence of earned merchandising — the budtenders or store managers picked this brand to promote.

Why it matters. Featured share is one of the only observable signals that distinguishes "brand we stock" from "brand we recommend." Curaleaf brands featured in 1.03% of pilot observations — consistent with mid-tier endorsement, slightly above the field average.

Phase 2 enhancement. We can mine the menu's section-header text to surface more editorial signal — promotional carousels, brand-of-the-month banners, custom category pages. That's a v2 feature: more signal, same architecture.
