Which retailers are off-band this week?

Until Curaleaf provides MSRP, we model Synthetic MSRP from two anchors: (1) median price at Curaleaf-owned stores (ground truth) when N≥3 doors carry the matched SKU, otherwise (2) cross-retailer modal price. Products are matched on brand × product type × pack size × THC band. Retailers are flagged at four severity bands (±5/10/15/25%) against the Synthetic MSRP.

Methodology · Synthetic MSRP construction

How we model implied MSRP without an MSRP feed
RuleAnchorMinimumConfidence
1. Curaleaf-owned modal Median price at Curaleaf-owned stores carrying the matched SKU N ≥ 3 Curaleaf-owned doors HIGH (Curaleaf-owned)
2. Cross-retailer modal (Circana convention) Median across all non-Curaleaf retailers carrying matched SKU N ≥ 10 obs & ≥ 3 distinct retailers HIGH (market modal)
3. Trimmed-mean fallback 10% trimmed mean (trim 10% from each tail) N ≥ 10 obs (no modal ≥ 30%) MEDIUM
4. Multi-state escalation Pilot-wide modal across IL/NJ/NY State N < 10 obs MEDIUM (thin market)
5. Persistence rule (Nielsen RMS convention) Synthetic MSRP does not change unless new modal persists 8 weeks — Anti-drift

Matching rules. brand_canonical (Curaleaf graph: Select, Grassroots, Find, Anthem, JAMS, Reef, B Noble); product_type (vape / flower / pre-roll / edible / concentrate, Headset-derived taxonomy); pack_size (weight_g in grams or size_mg in milligrams, parsed at extraction); THC band (quintile bins per category, e.g. flower 18–22% / 22–26% / 26–30% / 30%+). Unmatched cohorts are excluded from MSRP modeling — they appear in distribution counts but not in pricing compliance.

Severity bands. ±5% = in-band (within typical weekly fluctuation, not flagged). ±5-10% = soft excursion. ±10-15% = hard excursion (fires P2 alert). ±15-25% = severe (fires P1 alert). >±25% = extreme (fires P0 alert). Both above-band and below-band excursions are reported — overpricing erodes velocity, underpricing erodes margin.

▸ Once Curaleaf provides MSRP

The Synthetic MSRP becomes the fallback, not the primary anchor. Real MSRP plugs into the same severity-band framework on the same page. The Curaleaf-owned modal becomes a secondary read — useful for detecting whether Curaleaf retail itself is on-MSRP. The architecture does not change; only the anchor source does.
