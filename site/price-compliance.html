Methodology · Synthetic MSRP construction

How we model implied MSRP without an MSRP feed
RuleAnchorMinimumConfidence
1. Curaleaf-owned modalMedian price at Curaleaf-owned stores carrying the matched SKUN ≥ 3 Curaleaf-owned storesHIGH (Curaleaf-owned)
2. Cross-retailer modalMedian across all non-Curaleaf retailers carrying matched SKUN ≥ 10 obs & ≥ 3 distinct retailersHIGH (market modal)
3. Trimmed-mean fallback10% trimmed mean (trim 10% from each tail)N ≥ 10 obs (no modal ≥ 30%)MEDIUM
4. Multi-state escalationPilot-wide modal across IL/NJ/NYState N < 10 obsMEDIUM (thin market)
5. Persistence ruleSynthetic MSRP does not change unless new modal persists 8 weeks—Anti-drift

Matching rules. brand_canonical (Curaleaf graph: Select, Grassroots, Find, Anthem, JAMS, Reef, B Noble); product_type (vape / flower / pre-roll / edible / concentrate); pack_size (weight_g or size_mg, parsed at extraction); THC band (quintile bins per category, e.g. flower 18-22% / 22-26% / 26-30% / 30%+). Unmatched cohorts are excluded from MSRP modeling.

Positioning. Each cohort is labeled above-market, below-market, at-market, or no market baseline (insufficient market observations to compare), based on the delta between the Curaleaf-owned median and the market median.

▸ Once Curaleaf provides MSRP

The Synthetic MSRP becomes the fallback, not the primary anchor. Real MSRP plugs into the same positioning framework on the same page. The Curaleaf-owned modal becomes a secondary read — useful for detecting whether Curaleaf retail itself is on-MSRP. The architecture does not change; only the anchor source does.
